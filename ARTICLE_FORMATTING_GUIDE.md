# 📰 Article Formatting Guide - WorldNow Blog

## How to Format Your Blog Articles

This guide will help you add professional styling and formatting to your articles using simple markdown syntax. Follow these instructions to make your articles look great!

---

## 1. Sub Headings (### Format)

### What It Does
Creates a styled sub-heading with a red underline. Perfect for breaking your article into sections.

### How to Use
Type `###` followed by a space and your heading text:

```
### Your Heading Here
```

### Example
```
This is the main article body.

### The Problem
Here you explain the problem in detail...

### The Solution
Here you present your solution...

### Conclusion
Wrap up your thoughts here...
```

### How It Looks
- **Font**: Large, bold, serif style (professional newspaper look)
- **Color**: Dark text (#0d0d0d)
- **Border**: Red bottom border
- **Spacing**: Automatically adds top margin (1.5rem) and bottom margin (0.75rem)

---

## 2. Bullet Points & Lists

### What It Does
Creates organized bullet point or numbered lists for easy reading.

### How to Use - Bullet Points
Use asterisk `*` for each item:

```
* First point here
* Second point here
* Third point here
```

### How to Use - Numbered Lists
Use `1.` for each item:

```
1. First step
2. Second step
3. Third step
```

### Example
```
### Key Benefits

* Improved readability
* Professional presentation
* Better user engagement
* Easy to scan information

### Steps to Follow

1. Write your main content
2. Add sub headings with ###
3. Add bullet points where needed
4. Publish your article
```

### How It Looks
- **Style**: Bullet or numbered format
- **Color**: Gray text
- **Font Size**: Slightly smaller than body text
- **Spacing**: Proper indentation and gaps between items
- **Alignment**: Vertically stacked with proper gaps

---

## 3. Regular Paragraphs

### What It Does
Creates standard text paragraphs for your main content.

### How to Use
Simply type your text normally. **Separate paragraphs with a blank line**:

```
This is the first paragraph. You can write as much as you want here and it will automatically wrap to the next line.

This is the second paragraph. Just leave a blank line between paragraphs and the system will handle the rest automatically.

This is the third paragraph.
```

### How It Looks
- **Font**: Serif font (professional newspaper style)
- **Font Size**: Medium (responsive - larger on desktop, smaller on mobile)
- **Color**: Dark gray/black
- **Alignment**: Justified text (aligns on both left and right)
- **Line Height**: 1.8 (extra space between lines for easy reading)
- **Spacing**: Automatic margin between paragraphs

## 5. Best Practices & Tips

### ✅ DO's:
- ✅ Use `###` to break your article into logical sections
- ✅ Use bullet points for lists of items or features
- ✅ Use numbered lists for step-by-step instructions
- ✅ Start with a strong opening paragraph
- ✅ Keep paragraphs focused on one idea
- ✅ Use sub headings frequently (every 2-3 paragraphs)

### ❌ DON'Ts:
- ❌ Use `#` or `##` (only `###` works)
- ❌ Forget to add a blank line between paragraphs
- ❌ Write very long paragraphs (break them up with headings)
- ❌ Mix bullet points and numbered lists without separating them
- ❌ Add extra spaces or formatting - keep it simple!

## 9. Common Questions

**Q: Can I use bold or italic text?**
A: Currently, simple styling isn't available. We keep formatting simple for consistency.

**Q: What if I want more than 3 levels of headings?**
A: Use `###` for all sub headings. This keeps the hierarchy simple and consistent.

**Q: Can I add links?**
A: Links to tags are automatically added at the bottom. Direct URL linking in content isn't supported yet.

**Q: How many sub headings should I use?**
A: Use them every 2-3 paragraphs. A good rule: if a section is more than 200 words, it needs a sub heading.

**Q: Do blank lines between paragraphs matter?**
A: YES! The system uses blank lines to identify where one paragraph ends and another begins. Always use them!
